1. Fundamentals: How to Calculate a Percentage

The term "percent" comes from the Latin per centum, meaning "by the hundred." To calculate a basic percentage, you are determining the ratio of a part to a whole.

The Basic Percentage Formula

To find what percentage \(X\) is of \(Y\), use the following formula:

$$\text{Percentage} = \left( \frac{\text{Part}}{\text{Whole}} \right) \times 100$$
  • Example: What percentage is 25 out of 200?
  • Calculation: \((25 \div 200) \times 100 = 12.5\%\)

Finding the Value of a Percentage

To find a specific percentage of a known number:

$$\text{Value} = \text{Whole} \times \left( \frac{\text{Percentage}}{100} \right)$$
  • Example: What is 15% of $200?
  • Calculation: \(200 \times 0.15 = \$30\)

2. Tracking Growth: Percentage Change (Increase & Decrease)

Percentage change is used to measure the relative difference between an old value and a new value over time. This is common in finance, population studies, and retail.

The Percent Change Formula

While often viewed as two different tools, both rely on the same logic:

$$\text{Percentage Change} = \left( \frac{V_{new} - V_{old}}{V_{old}} \right) \times 100$$
Pro Tip: A positive result indicates a percentage increase, while a negative result indicates a percentage decrease.
  • Scenario (Increase): A price rises from $50 to $65. Calculation: \((\$65 - \$50) \div \$50 \times 100 = 30\%\).
  • Scenario (Decrease): A price drops from $800 to $600. Calculation: \((\$600 - \$800) \div \$800 \times 100 = -25\%\).

3. Comparison Logic: Percentage Difference

Unlike "change," which looks at one value over time, percentage difference compares two different values that are of the same category (e.g., comparing the daily calories of two different diets).

Percentage Difference Formula

Because neither value is considered the "original," we use the average as the reference point:

$$\text{Percentage Difference} = \left( \frac{|V_1 - V_2|}{\frac{V_1 + V_2}{2}} \right) \times 100$$

Example: Compare 90 and 100. The difference is 10. The average is 95. \((10 \div 95) \times 100 = 10.53\%\).

4. Business & Finance: Profit Margins

In business, percentages are the language of profitability. Our calculator includes a Margin Calculator function to determine the health of your revenue.

Gross Profit Percentage Formula

$$\text{Gross Profit \%} = \left( \frac{\text{Revenue} - \text{COGS}}{\text{Revenue}} \right) \times 100$$

Example: If your revenue is $200,000 and your Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is $150,000, your gross profit is $50,000. Calculation: \((\$50,000 \div \$200,000) \times 100 = 25\%\).

6. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Percentage and Percentage Points?

A percentage is a ratio of a total. Percentage points represent the simple arithmetic difference between two percentages. If an interest rate moves from 5% to 7%, it has increased by 2 percentage points, but it is a 40% increase in the rate itself.

How do I calculate 15% of a total?

To calculate 15% of any number, multiply the total by 0.15. For example, 15% of $250 is \(\$250 \times 0.15 = \$37.50\).

How do you calculate percentage increase between two numbers?

Subtract the original number from the new number, divide that result by the original number, and multiply by 100. The mathematical formula is \(\frac{V_{new} - V_{old}}{V_{old}} \times 100\).
